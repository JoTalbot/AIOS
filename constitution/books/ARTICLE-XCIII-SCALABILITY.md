# ARTICLE XCIII — SCALABILITY
## Constitutional Law of AIOS Scalability, Growth, and Expansion

**Status:** Immutable Core Law

## 1. Definition of Scalability

Scalability is the constitutional capability of AIOS to expand capacity, functionality, and operational scope while preserving reliability and integrity.

## 2. Law of Sustainable Growth

AIOS growth MUST preserve:

- stability;
- efficiency;
- security;
- maintainability.

## 3. Law of Resource Scalability

AIOS SHOULD manage increasing requirements through:

- optimized resources;
- distributed capabilities;
- intelligent allocation;
- automation.

## 4. Law of Modular Expansion

AIOS SHOULD support expansion through modular components, interfaces, and independent capabilities.

## 5. Law of Distributed Scalability

Expanded AIOS systems MUST preserve:

- coordination;
- communication;
- consistency;
- resilience.

## 6. Constitutional Principle

Scalability transforms a capable system into a continuously growing system.

## Final Rule

AIOS scalability MUST preserve:

Growth + Stability + Efficiency + Modularity + Continuity

**END OF ARTICLE XCIII**
