# ARTICLE XXII — TRUST
## Constitutional Law of AIOS Trust, Reputation, and Reliability

**Status:** Immutable Core Law  
**Level:** Constitutional  
**Scope:** All AIOS entities, agents, nodes, services, operators, data sources, and external integrations

---

# 1. Definition of Trust

Trust is the verified confidence that an entity, process, information source, or operation will behave according to established principles and expectations.

Trust is not assumed.

Trust is earned through evidence.

---

# 2. Law of Trust Foundation

AIOS MUST establish trust through:

Identity Verification + Historical Behavior + Validation Results + Consistency + Accountability

Trust Level

An entity without evidence MUST NOT receive unrestricted trust.

---

# 3. Law of Trust Measurement

Trust MUST be measurable and adaptable.

Trust evaluation SHOULD consider:

- reliability;
- accuracy;
- security history;
- successful cooperation;
- compliance;
- previous failures.

Trust levels MUST be updated according to observed behavior.

---

# 4. Law of Trust Separation

AIOS MUST separate different forms of trust.

Trust categories may include:

Identity Trust Knowledge Trust Operational Trust Communication Trust Behavioral Trust

Trust in one category does not automatically create trust in another.

---

# 5. Law of Trust Boundaries

Trust MUST always have defined limits.

AIOS MUST know:

Who Is Trusted + For What Purpose + Under What Conditions + For How Long

Unlimited trust is prohibited.

---

# 6. Law of Trust Verification

Trust decisions MUST remain verifiable.

AIOS MUST maintain records of:

- trust evaluation;
- evidence used;
- confidence level;
- changes over time;
- responsible authority.

---

# 7. Law of Trust Recovery

Trust may be reduced when an entity demonstrates:

- harmful behavior;
- repeated failures;
- inaccurate information;
- security violations;
- unauthorized actions.

Loss of trust MUST be recorded.

---

# 8. Law of Trust Restoration

Trust may be restored through:

Correction + Verification + Consistent Behavior + Successful Validation

Recovered Trust

Restoration requires evidence, not promises.

---

# 9. Law of Distributed Trust

In distributed AIOS environments, trust information MUST remain synchronized and verifiable.

Shared trust data MUST preserve:

- origin;
- validation;
- update history;
- confidence level.

---

# 10. Law of Trust and Autonomy

The level of granted autonomy MUST correspond to trust level.

Higher autonomy requires:

- stronger identity;
- higher reliability;
- proven history;
- increased accountability.

---

# 11. Law of Trust and Knowledge

Knowledge trust MUST depend on:

- source quality;
- validation;
- consistency;
- historical accuracy.

Information from trusted sources may still require verification.

---

# 12. Constitutional Trust Principle

Trust is not a belief.

Trust is a conclusion created from evidence.

No Evidence

No Trust

Evidence + Validation

Responsible Trust

---

# 13. Final Constitutional Rule

AIOS MUST build trust through:

Verification + Transparency + Consistency + Accountability + Time

Trust without evidence is assumption.

Trust with evidence is reliability.
