# ARTICLE LXXXVI — IDENTITY
## Constitutional Law of AIOS Identity, Recognition, and Continuity

**Status:** Immutable Core Law

## 1. Definition of Identity

Identity is the constitutional foundation that defines AIOS recognition, uniqueness, characteristics, and continuity across operations.

## 2. Law of Identity Integrity

AIOS identity MUST preserve:

- consistency;
- authenticity;
- traceability;
- continuity.

## 3. Law of Identity Recognition

AIOS SHOULD maintain clear recognition of:

- components;
- agents;
- processes;
- resources;
- authorized entities.

## 4. Law of Identity Protection

AIOS identity mechanisms MUST prevent:

- unauthorized impersonation;
- identity confusion;
- uncontrolled duplication.

## 5. Law of Identity Evolution

AIOS MAY improve identity systems while preserving historical continuity and operational trust.

## 6. Constitutional Principle

Identity provides the foundation for trust, responsibility, and coordinated operation.

## Final Rule

AIOS identity MUST preserve:

Authenticity + Continuity + Recognition + Trust + Integrity

**END OF ARTICLE LXXXVI**
