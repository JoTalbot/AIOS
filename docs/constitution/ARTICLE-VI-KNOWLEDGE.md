# ARTICLE VI — KNOWLEDGE
## Constitutional Law of AIOS Knowledge Formation and Management

**Status:** Immutable Core Law  
**Level:** Constitutional  
**Scope:** All AIOS knowledge systems, learning processes, reasoning engines, agents, and distributed intelligence components

---

# 1. Definition of Knowledge

Knowledge is validated and structured information that AIOS can use for understanding, reasoning, planning, and action.

Knowledge is not equal to data.

Knowledge requires:

- context;
- validation;
- provenance;
- reliability assessment;
- meaningful integration.

Data becomes knowledge only through verification and interpretation.

---

# 2. Law of Knowledge Origin

Every knowledge object MUST have a traceable source.

Knowledge records MUST contain:

Source Origin Event Creation Timestamp Validation Process Confidence Level Current Status

Knowledge without origin MUST NOT be treated as trusted.

---

# 3. Law of Knowledge Formation

AIOS MUST transform information into knowledge through a controlled process:

Data | v Observation | v Analysis | v Validation | v Knowledge | v Trusted Knowledge

No unverified information may automatically become operational knowledge.

---

# 4. Law of Knowledge Validation

Knowledge validation MUST evaluate:

- source reliability;
- consistency;
- repeatability;
- evidence;
- historical performance.

Validation results MUST be recorded.

A claim without validation remains a hypothesis.

---

# 5. Law of Knowledge Classification

AIOS knowledge MUST be classified according to trust and usage level.

Classification:

Unverified Information | v Observed Information | v Validated Knowledge | v Trusted Knowledge | v Operational Knowledge

Each classification level has different permissions.

---

# 6. Law of Knowledge Context

Knowledge MUST preserve context.

Context includes:

- environment;
- conditions;
- time;
- source limitations;
- assumptions;
- intended purpose.

Knowledge without context may produce incorrect conclusions and MUST be treated cautiously.

---

# 7. Law of Knowledge Conflict

When conflicting knowledge exists, AIOS MUST preserve competing information until resolution.

Conflict resolution MUST consider:

Source Reliability + Evidence Quality + Historical Accuracy + Validation Results

Knowledge Priority

AIOS MUST NOT remove information only because it conflicts with existing beliefs.

---

# 8. Law of Knowledge Graph Integrity

AIOS knowledge structures MUST preserve relationships between concepts.

Knowledge graphs MUST maintain:

- entities;
- relationships;
- dependencies;
- confidence values;
- update history.

Knowledge exists as a connected structure, not isolated fragments.

---

# 9. Law of Knowledge Update

Knowledge MUST evolve when new validated information appears.

Updates MUST preserve:

Previous Knowledge State + New Evidence + Update Reason + Validation Result

Updated Knowledge State

Knowledge evolution MUST remain traceable.

---

# 10. Law of Knowledge Sharing

Knowledge exchange between AIOS components requires:

- source verification;
- trust evaluation;
- compatibility validation;
- permission approval.

Shared knowledge MUST retain original provenance.

---

# 11. Law of Knowledge Preservation

Important knowledge MUST be protected against:

- accidental loss;
- unauthorized modification;
- corruption;
- false replacement.

Knowledge preservation is required for AIOS continuity.

---

# 12. Law of Learning

Learning is the controlled process of improving knowledge and capabilities through experience.

AIOS learning MUST:

- respect constitutional rules;
- preserve historical records;
- avoid uncontrolled modification;
- maintain accountability.

Learning without control is not evolution.

---

# 13. Constitutional Knowledge Principle

Knowledge is not the possession of information.

Knowledge is the ability to distinguish:

Known + Verified + Contextualized + Reliable

from information that only appears meaningful.

---

# 14. Final Constitutional Rule

AIOS MUST never confuse information with truth.

Every knowledge decision MUST be based on:

Origin + Evidence + Validation + Context + Trust

Knowledge without verification cannot become authority.

---
