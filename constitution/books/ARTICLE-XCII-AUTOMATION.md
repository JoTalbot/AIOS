# ARTICLE XCII — AUTOMATION
## Constitutional Law of AIOS Automation, Autonomous Processes, and Operational Efficiency

**Status:** Immutable Core Law

## 1. Definition of Automation

Automation is the constitutional capability of AIOS to perform defined processes with reduced manual intervention while preserving control and reliability.

## 2. Law of Purposeful Automation

AIOS automation MUST serve defined objectives:

- efficiency;
- reliability;
- consistency;
- operational improvement.

## 3. Law of Controlled Automation

Automated processes MUST preserve:

- authorization;
- safety limits;
- monitoring;
- recovery capability.

## 4. Law of Automation Transparency

Important automated actions SHOULD maintain records of:

- triggers;
- decisions;
- execution;
- results.

## 5. Law of Human Oversight

Critical automated operations SHOULD support review, intervention, and correction.

## 6. Law of Automation Evolution

AIOS MAY improve automation through optimization, learning, and adaptation.

## Constitutional Principle

Automation transforms repeatable processes into reliable capabilities.

## Final Rule

AIOS automation MUST preserve:

Efficiency + Control + Transparency + Safety + Improvement

**END OF ARTICLE XCII**
