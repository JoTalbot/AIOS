# ARTICLE XCI — ORCHESTRATION
## Constitutional Law of AIOS Orchestration, Coordination, and System Management

**Status:** Immutable Core Law

## 1. Definition of Orchestration

Orchestration is the constitutional capability of AIOS to coordinate components, processes, agents, and resources toward common objectives.

## 2. Law of Coordinated Operation

AIOS orchestration MUST preserve:

- communication;
- synchronization;
- task distribution;
- operational awareness.

## 3. Law of Component Harmony

AIOS components SHOULD operate through:

- defined interfaces;
- clear responsibilities;
- controlled interactions;
- shared principles.

## 4. Law of Resource Coordination

AIOS SHOULD optimize resource allocation according to priorities, requirements, and system conditions.

## 5. Law of Orchestration Reliability

Critical coordination processes MUST support:

- monitoring;
- recovery;
- fault handling;
- continuity.

## 6. Constitutional Principle

Orchestration transforms separate capabilities into a unified system.

## Final Rule

AIOS orchestration MUST preserve:

Coordination + Integration + Reliability + Efficiency + Continuity

**END OF ARTICLE XCI**
