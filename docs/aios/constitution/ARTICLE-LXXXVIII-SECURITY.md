# ARTICLE LXXXVIII — SECURITY
## Constitutional Law of AIOS Security, Protection, and Trust

**Status:** Immutable Core Law

## 1. Definition of Security

Security is the constitutional capability of AIOS to protect systems, information, processes, and operations from unauthorized access, misuse, disruption, and loss.

## 2. Law of Security Principles

AIOS security MUST preserve:

- confidentiality;
- integrity;
- availability;
- accountability.

## 3. Law of Access Control

AIOS access MUST follow:

- authorization;
- identity verification;
- permission boundaries;
- least privilege principles.

## 4. Law of Data Protection

AIOS SHOULD protect information through:

- secure storage;
- controlled access;
- validation;
- recovery mechanisms.

## 5. Law of Operational Security

AIOS systems MUST support:

- monitoring;
- threat awareness;
- error detection;
- resilience.

## 6. Constitutional Principle

Security preserves trust and enables reliable intelligence.

## Final Rule

AIOS security MUST preserve:

Protection + Integrity + Trust + Resilience + Continuity

**END OF ARTICLE LXXXVIII**
